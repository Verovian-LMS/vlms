
# Medical Education Glossary

This glossary provides definitions for common terms used in medical education and within the MedMaster platform.

## A

**Accreditation**: The process by which an educational program or institution is officially recognized and approved by an authoritative body.

**Active Learning**: Educational approach that encourages learners to engage directly with the material through activities rather than passively receiving information.

## B

**Blended Learning**: Educational approach that combines online digital media with traditional classroom methods.

**Board Certification**: The process by which a physician demonstrates mastery of their specialty's core competencies through examination.

## C

**CME (Continuing Medical Education)**: Educational activities that serve to maintain, develop, or increase knowledge, skills, and professional performance for healthcare professionals.

**Clinical Reasoning**: The process by which healthcare practitioners collect cues, process information, understand a patient problem, plan and implement interventions, evaluate outcomes, and reflect on the process.

**Cohort**: A group of students who work through a curriculum together.

## D

**Dashboard**: The personalized home screen in MedMaster that displays your enrolled courses, progress, and recommendations.

**Didactic**: Intended to teach or educate; often refers to lecture-based instruction.

## E

**Evidence-Based Medicine (EBM)**: The conscientious, explicit, and judicious use of current best evidence in making decisions about the care of individual patients.

## F

**Flipped Classroom**: Educational approach where students review lecture materials at home and engage in exercises and discussions during class time.

## H

**High-Yield**: Content that is most likely to be relevant for examinations or clinical practice.

## L

**Learning Objective**: A statement that describes what a learner should be able to do upon completion of a learning activity.

**Lecture**: A formal presentation delivered by an instructor, typically as part of a course module.

## M

**Module**: A self-contained unit of study within a course that focuses on a specific topic or competency.

**MCAT (Medical College Admission Test)**: A standardized examination for prospective medical students in the United States and Canada.

## O

**OSCE (Objective Structured Clinical Examination)**: An assessment method based on observed performance in simulated clinical scenarios.

## P

**Pathophysiology**: The study of the functional changes associated with or resulting from disease or injury.

**Progress Tracking**: Feature that monitors and displays a user's advancement through course material.

## R

**Residency**: Postgraduate medical training where physicians practice medicine under the supervision of fully licensed physicians.

## S

**Spaced Repetition**: Learning technique that incorporates increasing intervals of time between subsequent review of previously learned material.

**Syllabus**: An outline or summary of the main points of a course of study.

## U

**USMLE (United States Medical Licensing Examination)**: A three-step examination for medical licensure in the United States.

## W

**Webinar**: A live or recorded online educational presentation, workshop, or lecture conducted over the internet.
